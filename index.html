Know Your Therapist

The Fundamentals

Dr. Swetha Turlapati, Psychotherapist by experiential learning. Masters of Counseling Psychology and a Doctor of Pharmacy (Pharm D) with a postgraduate degree.

I am a trained and certified practitioner in mindfulness, narrative, and IMAGO (couple therapy) approaches. I’m informed by trauma in my sessions with clients. Other trainings include queer affirmative counseling practice (QACP), solution-focused brief therapy (SFBT), and Community Mental Health.

Therapeutic Relationship

I believe that collaboration and presence are at the core of a therapeutic relationship. Vulnerability, making space for moment-to-moment emotions, and sensitivity toward culture and systemic oppression are integral to my work.

My Vitamins

Co-learning spaces are what I look forward to in my journey as a therapist. I integrate client stories with theory, reflection, and supervision. My background as a patient counselor helps me hold awareness of the interaction between physical and mental health.

Calling Towards Communities

Community mental health has always been an integral part of my work. I have worked with NGOs, hospitals, schools, colleges, and corporates. Topics include stress management, mental health awareness, women’s mental health, mindful living, anxiety, depression, and medically unexplained symptoms.

I Wake Up To

Developing a connection to the client’s inner world — mind and body — brings calmness to my practice. I believe that conversation with oneself leads to one’s authentic, higher self.

Stories

I scheduled a session with Swetha at one of the most turbulent times in my life. I cried and laughed with her. Over two years, she helped me understand anxiety, panic, and myself. Therapy, for me, became a space to find tools — not answers — and that made all the difference.

Dr. Swetha is not just a wonderful psychologist, she is a very good human being. Her therapy helped me understand my thinking patterns and guided me toward healing from anxiety and depression.

She is an extremely patient listener who helps you realise the root cause of what you feel. That clarity itself is powerful.

I never believed therapy could help addiction — until I met Dr. Swetha. The Pause for Perspective is a must-visit space for the modern human.

It felt very comfortable speaking with her. She gave me space, asked relevant questions, and helped me organise my thoughts. Every minute felt worth it.
